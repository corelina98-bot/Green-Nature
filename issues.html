What is pollution?

Pollution is a global problem. Although urban areas are usually more polluted than the countryside, pollution can spread to remote places where no people live. Pollution is the introduction of harmful materials into the environment. These harmful materials are called pollutants. These pollutants damage the quality of air, water, and land.

Let's see the different types of pollution:

Water pollution

Pollutants seep into waterways, harming aquatic ecosystems. For instance, pesticides chemical poisons used to kill weeds and insects can contaminate water and harm wildlife. When water resources are polluted, all forms of life are threatened.

Land pollution

Landfills collect garbage and other land pollutants in a central location. Unfortunately, many places are running out of space for landfills. Industries, homes, and agricultural practices generate garbage, sewage, and chemical runoff that can pollute the land.
